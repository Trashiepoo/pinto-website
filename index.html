What is Muay Thai?

Muay Thai is referred to as “The Art of 8 Limbs”: using 8 points of contact- both hands, both elbows, both knees, and both shins to damage and/or injure your opponent. Muay Thai or Thai Boxing is the national sport and cultural martial art of Thailand and was developed several hundreds of years ago as a form of close combat during times of war.

Today Muay Thai as a sport is becoming exceedingly popular on a global scale and has recently been accepted as an Olympic sport. Professional martial artists from all sides agree Muay Thai is essential to becoming an all-around multifaceted fighter. Watching a Muay Thai fight is truly a beautiful thing, there is a respected tradition throughout the whole event from the head pieces worn by fighters “Mongkon” to the “sealing of the ring” to the traditional and ritualistic dance “Wai Kru/Ram Muay” performed before the fight.

Muay Thai is loved by many for its colorful and rich history and the way it unites the world in friendly competition. A beautiful display of basic techniques that are constantly practiced and put to the test makes this sport what it is. Nak Muays are always learning and evolving, and it is our job to help grow the sport of Muay Thai in America.

CLASSES

Muay Thai class is designed for all experience levels and created to give the student a basic understanding of proper techniques and concepts. Muay Thai class will not have hard sparring, however, there will be live drills and light sparring. If it is your goal to fight feel free to speak with


What you can expect for your first class: When you arrive please check in at front desk, you will sign a waiver before your class and then Suzy will show you around the gym and introduce you to your Coach for the class. During the warm up one of our coaches will come around and go over any basic techniques/fundamentals you will need to know for that specific class session. The key is to have fun!


Dress code: Your choice of clean active wear that does not restrict movement.

Muay Thai shorts recommended but not required. No shoes or socks on the mat.

Gym bag: Please bring hand wraps, gloves, shin pads, water, a towel for sweat. If needed inquire for gear recommendations.

JR MUAY THAI (7-12)

This is a 45-minute class is for ages 7-12 years old. In this class we will teach the fundamentals of Muay Thai. Junior Muay Thai focuses on teaching our students to be disciplined and respectful. Watch as your student develops their physical abilities and increase mental focus while making new friends and having fun.


Please call to schedule your child/children for a FREE trial.


Dress code: Your choice of active wear that does not restrict movement. Muay Thai shorts are recommended but not required. No shoes or socks on the mat.

Gym bag: hand wraps, boxing gloves, shin pads

MUAY THAI HIIT (COED)

This is a 45 minute coed high intensity interval training bag class using Muay Thai techniques to get the heart rate going! A fast-paced class that is beginner friendly.


Dress code: Active wear that does not restrict movement. No shoes or socks on the mat.

Gear needed: hand wraps, boxing gloves

PRO MUAY THAI

The Pro class is for people who compete from amateur level to pros or anyone looking to train at a high level. It is invite only and will have a basic structure that includes shadow boxing, pad work, sparring and clinch work. Expect this class to be an hour to one and half hours long. You must be invited to this class in order to attend, please inquire if you are interested and based on your experience level, we will let you know if you may attend. If you do attend, you are responsible to bring your own protective gear and understand “sparring etiquette” we have a zero-tolerance policy for sparring getting out of hand. You will be asked to leave and not come back.


Dress code: active wear that does not restrict movement. Muay Thai shorts recommended.

Required gear: bag gloves 10-12ozs, sparring gloves 16oz, shin pads, knee pads, mouth guard- if you do not have these things you cannot spar. groin protector (optional but recommended)
